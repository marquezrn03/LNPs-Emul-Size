1. Analyzing Correlations in LNP-Stabilized Emulsions

Limitations of Simple Models

Initial analysis using the Lignin Coverage Number ($Lc \propto \frac{C_{LNP} \cdot D_{emulsion}}{d_{LNP}}$) revealed significant data scatter when plotted against droplet size ($D_{emulsion}$). This indicates that while $Lc$ captures the basic geometric concept of particle coverage, it fails to account for the dominant influence of other critical factors in these complex systems.

Key confounding variables include:

  • Lignin Heterogeneity & Modification: Different sources (Kraft, Alkali, etc.) and chemical modifications (acetylation, sulfonation, grafting) drastically alter interfacial behavior.
  • pH Sensitivity: LNP charge, aggregation, and solubility are highly pH-dependent, affecting stability mechanisms beyond simple coverage.
  • Kinetic Effects & Energy Input: Emulsification method and energy input influence initial droplet size and particle adsorption kinetics.
  • Measurement Variability: Differences in LNP and droplet size measurement techniques across studies introduce noise.

Refined Analysis Strategy

To obtain potentially clearer correlations, we can explore the relationship suggested by dimensional analysis, $D_{emulsion} \propto d_{LNP} / C_{LNP}$, for different subsets of the data available in the Marquez et al. review. We plot droplet size ($D_{emulsion}$) against the combined parameter $X = d_{LNP} / C_{LNP}$ and apply a power-law trendline ($D = a \cdot X^b$) to quantify the correlation ($R^2$) for the currently filtered data.

Use the filters below to select specific conditions. The "Apply Clean Subset" button provides a recommended starting point by filtering for unmodified lignins in the optimal pH range (4-7) and excluding very large LNPs (>2000nm), which often yields a better correlation. However, you can apply the trendline to any filtered subset.

Note: The $R^2$ value indicates how well the power-law model fits the selected data subset. A low $R^2$ for a given filter combination highlights the influence of factors not captured by the simple $d_{LNP}/C_{LNP}$ ratio.
